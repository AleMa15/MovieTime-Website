List of Drama Movies

  • The Godfather (1972)

    The aging patriarch of an organized crime dynasty transfers control of his empire to his reluctant son.

  • Schindler's List (1993)

    A German businessman saves the lives of more than a thousand Jewish refugees during the Holocaust by employing them in his factories.

  • The Shawshank Redemption (1994)

    Two imprisoned men forge a deep friendship and find solace through acts of common decency.

  • Forrest Gump (1994)

    A simple man with a low IQ navigates through decades of American history and finds success despite his limitations.

  • Titanic (1997)

    A wealthy young woman falls in love with a poor artist aboard the ill-fated maiden voyage of the "unsinkable" RMS Titanic.

  • A Beautiful Mind (2001)

    The true story of a brilliant but troubled mathematician who struggles with schizophrenia and learns to overcome his mental illness.

  • The Help (2011)

    A young journalist in 1960s Mississippi interviews African American maids and shares their stories, sparking a movement for social change.

  • The Social Network (2010)

    The founding of Facebook and the legal battles that followed, as Mark Zuckerberg and his associates become multi-billionaires.

  • The Fault in Our Stars (2014)

    Two teenage cancer patients fall in love and embark on a journey to fulfill their dreams, despite the obstacles in their way.

  • The Green Mile (1999)

    The story of a death row corrections officer who becomes deeply affected by a seemingly gentle inmate with supernatural powers.

  • The King's Speech (2010)

    The story of King George VI's struggle to overcome a debilitating stutter with the help of a speech therapist.

  • Goodfellas (1990)

    The rise and fall of a mob associate in New York City, as he navigates the dangerous world of organized crime.

  • The English Patient (1996)

    The story of a badly burned man who recounts his life in flashbacks, including a doomed love affair during World War II.

  • American Beauty (1999)

    A man in the midst of a mid-life crisis begins to reevaluate his life, his marriage, and his place in the world.

  • Dead Poets Society (1989)

    An unorthodox English teacher inspires his students to pursue their dreams and embrace the beauty of literature.

  • Mona Lisa Smile (2003)

    A progressive art history teacher at an all-women's college challenges her students to question societal norms and find their own paths.

  • Loving Vincent (2017)

    An animated biographical drama about the life and death of Vincent van Gogh, told through his own artwork.

  • A Walk to Remember (2002)

    A popular high school boy falls in love with a quiet, religious girl, but their relationship is tested when she reveals a heartbreaking secret.

  • The Notebook (2004)

    A man reads a notebook to a woman with Alzheimer's, telling the story of their passionate and tumultuous love affair in the 1940s.

  • Brokeback Mountain (2005)

    The story of two cowboys who fall in love with each other in the 1960s and try to keep their relationship secret in a conservative rural community.
